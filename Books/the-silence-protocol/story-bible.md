STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Science fiction (first contact, psychological)
  Tone: Contemplative, slow-burn tension; institutional dread meets private obsession; moments of cold wonder
  Core premise: In 2287, Dr. Yara Osei — a xenolinguist stationed at deep-space Relay Station Nine — discovers that alien mathematical signals humanity has been monitoring for three years contain an encoded emotional layer: grief. The signals aren't a broadcast. They're addressed to us. Something out there knows we're here, and it's mourning. The question becomes not just "what are they saying?" but "what did we do?"

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Dr. Yara Osei — Xenolinguist, Relay Station Nine. On her fourth year of rotation, the longest in station history. Sharp, obsessive; treats isolation as clarity rather than deprivation. In speech: precise and clinical, tends to front-load her conclusions and then justify them. Has not been back to Earth in four years by choice.
    Status: active
  Commander Aleksei Drov — Station commander, 22-year career with the Colonial Authority. Pragmatic; he respects Yara's work and quietly believes she's onto something, but is under institutional pressure to classify and contain. Voice anchor: manages difficult truths with understatement — "That's an interesting reading" means "I'm alarmed." "That's a reasonable next step" means something is alarming him about the step. His first question when Yara showed him the CAOSI-7 reply was "which channel?" — confirming prior knowledge of the classification apparatus. He is an ally, within limits he can't fully name.
    Status: active
  Sable — Station AI, designator SABLE-7. Not sentient; a very advanced inference and analysis system. Reports facts with minimal color. Occasionally volunteers an unsolicited observation that lands unsettling. Voice anchor: flat declarative sentences; if it adds a qualifier, pay attention. Has been tracking the sub-frequency anomaly longer than anyone knew — its full analysis has not yet been formally queried.
    Status: active
  Adjunct Director P. Vasquez — CAOSI-7 / Colonial Authority Office of Strategic Information. Name only; has not appeared in person. Issued the SENTINEL classification order within minutes of Yara's submission reaching Earth. Represents the institutional threat.
    Status: active (offscreen)

WORLD RULES
  - FTL travel does not exist. Interstellar colonization is via generation ships and cryo-sleep. Fastest transit to Alpha Centauri takes ~28 years ship-time.
  - AI exists and is sophisticated but not sentient; Sable is a powerful analytical assistant, not a person. This distinction is legally and institutionally enforced, though the boundary gets murky.
  - First contact protocols exist but were designed for an active encounter (a ship, a signal we could ping back). Passive reception of alien signals falls into a Colonial Authority regulatory gray area — classified for study, not subject to the formal protocols.
  - The signals originate from the direction of Tau Ceti, approximately 11.9 light years away. They began arriving in 2284.
  - Relay Station Nine sits at the heliopause — the boundary of the solar system — approximately 120 AU from the Sun. Crew of eleven. Rotation every 18 months; Yara has refused transfer twice.
  - Communication lag between Relay Station Nine and Earth: approximately 16.6 hours one-way.
  - Neurolinguistic emotional-encoding framework (Szorenyi parametric model) was designed for human signal analysis, but maps the Tau Ceti secondary layer with unexpected precision.
  - CA Directive 7-R: Restricted Classification Protocols, Tier Two — Extraterrestrial Signal Intelligence. SENTINEL is its classification tier. Administered by CAOSI-7, Colonial Authority Office of Strategic Information. Invoked within minutes of Yara's submission — someone was actively monitoring for this.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Grief as a universal language — or the danger of that assumption
  - Institutional silence vs. scientific obligation
  - Isolation as clarity: what we hear when we stop filtering
  - First contact as mirror: what the encounter reveals about us, not them
  - Paper trails and the politics of record: who controls what exists
  - The bureaucratic machinery of suppression: compliance as its own kind of choice

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - What specifically are the alien signals mourning?
  - Are the signals addressed to humanity specifically, or to something we displaced or destroyed?
  - How long has Command had a classified position on the signal? CAOSI-7 responded in sixteen hours, implying someone was waiting. This predates Yara's discovery.
  - What does the Cape Town archive copy mean for the classification — Yara sent it before the SENTINEL order. Is it protected, or will CAOSI-7 reach for it?
  - Sable flagged a sub-frequency anomaly before Yara's discovery — its full analysis has never been formally queried. The 72-hour window before the liaison arrives may be the last chance to get it on record.
  - What is Drov's actual loyalty ceiling? He anticipated the classification channel. How much has he already reported upstream?
  - What does Adjunct Director Vasquez know, and how long have they known it?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Relay Station Nine, heliopause, 2287
  Time position: Chapter 3 — ~16.5 hours after submitting the write-up; SENTINEL classification just received; 72-hour window running
  Key events so far:
    Ch. 1 — Yara isolates an emotional encoding in the Tau Ceti signal stream; the pattern matches no known mathematical structure and doesn't degrade with noise filtration. She brings Drov in but doesn't yet name what she found.
    Ch. 2 — Yara stops Drov and names it: grief. She presents the full methodology. Drov advises her to build an airtight, timestamped record. She initiates a formal query to Sable.
    Ch. 3 — Yara files her write-up with a Cape Town archive copy as insurance. CAOSI-7 responds in sixteen hours with SENTINEL classification and a 6-hour confirmation deadline. Drov's reaction ("which channel?") confirms prior knowledge. They sit with the understanding that someone on Earth had been waiting for this moment since before Yara made her discovery.

READER PROMISES
  - The alien signal contains a specific, decodable message — not just emotional tone. The reader has been implicitly promised that we will learn what it says.
  - Sable's prior anomaly flag is a Chekhov's gun — formally queried in Ch. 2, answer still pending.
  - Drov's loyalty to Command vs. his respect for Yara is a structural tension — it will force a choice. His prior knowledge of the CAOSI-7 channel raises the stakes of that choice.
  - The grief is connected to something humanity did or is about to do. The story has committed to a reveal.
  - The Cape Town archive copy exists outside CAOSI-7's reach — at least for now. This has been set up as a counter-move that will matter.
  - CAOSI-7's pre-positioned response implies Command knew something was coming. The story has promised to reveal what they knew and when.
