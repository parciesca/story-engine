STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Hard sci-fi / philosophical epic
  Tone: Near-future thriller (Act I) → institutional reverence (Act II) →
    mythic (Acts III-V). Prose stays concrete even as timescales become
    incomprehensible. Beige UN rooms and espresso anchor early acts;
    institutional architecture and void-physics anchor later ones.
  Core premise: Humanity discovers the multiverse is a graveyard — every
    advanced civilization accidentally destroys its universe by mishandling
    vacuum energy. Humanity chooses restraint, builds institutions to hold
    that restraint across millions of years, and eventually joins a network
    of survivors to mend a multiverse slowly coming apart at the seams.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Dr. Lena Sarova — Astrophysicist, Cerro Paranal Deep Array. Discovers
    the anomaly (eleven seconds of data). Steady, precise, carries Act I
    on competence and restraint. Voice: clinical but not cold, translates
    wonder into measurement. Status: active (Act I era)
  Miguel Estrada — Senior technician, night shift observer. Builds the
    classification system (Class 1-5). Warm, functional, underused after
    Ch. 2. His taxonomy outlasts him. Status: active (Act I era)
  General Raines — US military liaison to the Coalition. The "honest
    general" who discloses Project Kindling against orders. His hallway
    motivation speech (Ch. 4) is the story's most human moment. Voice:
    controlled, precise, cracks only in private. Status: active (Act I era)
  Dr. Beatriz Melo — Philosopher/ethicist on the Coalition. The conscience.
    Asks the right question at the right time. Designs the Paradox
    Curriculum, which outlasts every other human contribution. Voice:
    Socratic, gentle, devastating. Status: active (Act I era)
  Director Tanabe — UN bureaucratic mover. Pen-tapping tic. Effective,
    functional, not deep. Status: active (Act I era)
  Wen Kaida — Researcher, Threshold Authority (Act II, ~2,200 years
    later). Discovers Fragment 7's substrate signal and the extraction
    field threading through all nearby universes. Her detective work is
    the story's best investigative sequence. Status: active (Act II era)
  Consul Vasić — Threshold Authority leadership. Makes the burial
    decision (the Kaida Constant). Clean, cold, correct — right and
    terrible simultaneously. Status: active (Act II era)
  Asante — Threshold Authority, opposes the burial. Argues for
    transparency. Loses the argument. Status: active (Act II era)
  Ilyen — Enne observer, Act III genesis detection. Less individually
    defined. Status: active (Act III era)
  Sable — Enne analyst, Act III. Distinctive analytical voice. Detects
    the second genesis event and Fragment 7's curriculum broadcast.
    Status: active (Act III era)
  The Consuls (collective) — The institution as character across Acts
    II-III. The Response Matrix and Hold Protocol are more compelling
    than any individual Consul. Status: institutional continuity
  Ry / Nath / Kiol — Enne contact team, Act IV. The eleven-minute wait
    is the scene; they're the frame around it. Status: active (Act IV era)
  The Ohn — Civilization; Region 5511-Ohn. Self-named "the students."
    3.5 million years since intervention. Philosophical, methodical.
    Study the curriculum's pedagogy rather than its physics. Best
    "character" in Acts IV-V despite being an entire species. Their
    observation that Fragment 7 is empty is devastating. Status: active
  The Kesh-an — Civilization; first bilateral contact. 4 million years
    developing in isolation. Resonance engineering specialists.
    Status: active
  The Leth-enne — Civilization; Region 2067-Leth. "The ones who almost
    didn't." Focus on field topology. Status: active
  The Taur — Civilization; Region 884-Taur. "The grateful." Youngest
    survivor, reckless creativity in substrate theory. Status: active

WORLD RULES
  - Big Bangs are caused by civilizations mishandling vacuum energy
    (crossing the "threshold"). Each genesis event destroys the parent
    universe and creates a new one.
  - The multiverse is a void containing universe-bubbles and fragment
    remnants of destroyed universes.
  - The void degrades with each genesis event — fabric frays, grows
    thinner, more fragile.
  - Fragment 7 is a remnant of an ancient civilization that mastered
    the threshold. Its creators are long dead. It operates as an
    automated monument.
  - Fragment 7's extraction field threads through all nearby universes
    (the Kaida Constant — discovered by Wen Kaida, buried by Vasić).
  - The extraction field draws energy from the void, including from
    humanity's universe.
  - Fragment 7 broadcasts a curriculum into every newborn universe at
    the moment of genesis — a layered teaching on how to handle vacuum
    energy safely.
  - The curriculum must be learned sequentially; understanding-before-
    capability is the design principle.
  - The extraction field can be repurposed for directed communication
    between universes.
  - The void can be healed through "breathing nodes" — restructured
    field elements that return energy to the medium.
  - Classification system (Miguel's taxonomy): Class 1-5 for universe
    remnants based on their state.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Intelligence as both gift and existential risk
  - Restraint as survival strategy — and its limits (avoidance vs. mastery)
  - Institutional patience vs. individual urgency
  - The teacher who outlives the teaching (Fragment 7's empty chair)
  - Isolation vs. collaboration as modes of survival
  - Legacy as infrastructure rather than memory
  - Students becoming teachers — the cycle of knowledge transmission
  - The monoculture problem: no single mind can solve everything;
    diversity of thought is structurally necessary

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
