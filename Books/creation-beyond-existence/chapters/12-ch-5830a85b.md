The fifty-generation Hold expired on a day that had no particular significance, in a civilization that had long since stopped marking time by days. The notification surfaced in the Threshold chamber the way a stone surfaces in a riverbed after the current shifts — not suddenly, but inevitably, carried upward by the slow, patient erosion of time doing what it always does.

The 4,767th Consul received it.

They had known it was coming. Every Consul in the chain had known, the way each link in a relay knows the baton is approaching — not the moment of arrival, but the certainty of it. Fifty Consuls had held the weight. Fifty Consuls had added their analysis, their doubts, their proposals, their rebuttals to the growing body of deliberation in the Matrix. Some had argued for immediate action upon the Hold's expiration. Some had argued for another Hold. Some had proposed disclosure frameworks of extraordinary nuance. Some had written brief, blunt entries that amounted to: *I don't know what to do. I trust my successor to be wiser.*

The 4,767th Consul read them all. Every entry. Every argument. Every counter-argument. The work of fifty minds, each one carrying the full weight of the Constant and the Fragment 7 revelation and the question that the Hold had been designed to answer: *What now?*

The Matrix's consensus — not unanimous, never unanimous, the institution was too honest for that — had converged on something that the 4,767th Consul found both terrifying and inevitable.

Contact.

Not disclosure to the *enne* at large. Not yet. The Matrix was clear on this: the *enne* would need to be prepared, gradually, over generations, for the knowledge that Fragment 7 was alive and purposeful and that the threshold could be crossed. The Paradox Curriculum would need to evolve. The cultural architecture of restraint would need to be strong enough to absorb the revelation without cracking. That was long-term work, measured in centuries.

But contact with Fragment 7 — cautious, controlled, initiated from the Threshold seat with the full institutional weight of the Authority behind it — that was the consensus. Because the fifty Consuls who had studied Ilyen's data had arrived, one after another, at the same conclusion: the *enne* could not remain alone forever. The multiverse was vast and old and full of the dead, and Fragment 7 was the only other living thing in it, and it had been trying to save civilizations for longer than the stars had burned, and it had been failing, and the *enne* were the only ones who had ever succeeded, and perhaps — *perhaps* — the two survivors had something to offer each other that neither could achieve alone.

The 4,767th Consul closed the Matrix. Opened a new file. Wrote:

*I am the Consul who lifts the Hold. What follows is my responsibility, and I accept it. The fifty minds before me have given me their best thinking. I will honor it by acting.*

They paused. Thought of Vasić, the second Consul, who had buried the truth to protect a species that was still learning how to survive. Thought of the 4,717th, who had invoked the Hold to give the institution time to be wise. Thought of all the unnamed minds in between, each one sitting in this same chamber, carrying the same weight, adding their thread to a tapestry that none of them would live to see completed.

*The Matrix recommends contact. I concur. But I will not do this from the seat alone.*

They activated the Consul notification protocol — the same one Ilyen had used, generations ago, the old bell in the old tower.

*Observatory, all stations. This is the Threshold seat. I am invoking Disclosure Protocol One, preparation phase. Over the coming years, the Paradox Curriculum will be revised to include new material regarding the nature and origin of the Kaida Constant. This is the beginning of a process, not its conclusion. Details to follow through institutional channels.*

*Additionally, I am establishing a new body under Consular authority: the Contact Commission. Its mandate is to develop, test, and when ready, execute a framework for initiating communication with an intelligence in the inter-universal void designated Fragment 7. Membership is open to any qualified enne researcher who, upon learning the full scope of the Constant's origin, is willing to carry that knowledge and work with it responsibly.*

*This is not an emergency. This is not a crisis. This is the culmination of 1.3 million years of institutional deliberation and fifty generations of focused analysis. We are ready.*

*The seat has decided. The work begins.*

The notification went out. The *enne* received it. And for the first time in their long, patient, careful history, they turned their attention not inward — not toward the threshold they had sworn to respect, not toward the survival they had built on restraint — but outward. Toward the void. Toward the ancient, tireless, failing teacher who had been their neighbor since before they were born.

Toward the possibility that survival was not the end of the story, but the beginning.
