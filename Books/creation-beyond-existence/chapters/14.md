The Commission's report arrived at the Threshold chamber in the form that institutional tradition demanded: a single unified document, authored collectively, stripped of individual attribution. The Commission spoke as one voice or not at all. This was a deliberate architectural choice from the founding charter — no single researcher's reputation or ambition could attach to recommendations this consequential.

The 4,767th Consul read it in the manner that had become ritual across their tenure: once quickly, for shape; once slowly, for substance; once more, for what was left unsaid.

The shape was clear. The Commission had identified a viable contact method — Vael's perturbation model, the knock on the pipe — and had begun decoding Fragment 7's curriculum, revealing it to be not the warning the *enne* had always assumed but an education. A path through the threshold rather than away from it. The Commission recommended two parallel actions: execute the perturbation to initiate contact, and begin structured study of the curriculum's deeper layers to develop the theoretical foundation for substrate mastery.

The substance was rigorous. Modeling data, linguistic analysis, risk assessments, historical context. Dort's civilizational framing was woven throughout — careful, measured, honest about what was at stake. The Commission did not minimize the implications. They were proposing that the *enne* reconsider the foundational principle of their civilization. They knew what they were asking.

What was left unsaid was what the Consul found most instructive. The report did not recommend a timeline. It did not argue for urgency. It presented the path and left the pace to the seat. The Commission understood, as its founding charter required it to understand, that the decision of *when* was not theirs to make.

The Consul opened the Matrix.

The section on substrate research was among the most heavily annotated in the entire archive. Generations of Consuls had contributed to it, and the thread that ran through their collective deliberation was not prohibition but caution of a very specific kind. The *enne* had survived by not pursuing substrate mastery. Fragment 7's curriculum suggested that mastery was possible and that avoidance was, in the long view, an incomplete strategy. But the transition from avoidance to mastery — the cultural, intellectual, and institutional shift required to walk a species across a line it had spent its entire conscious history behind — that transition was itself a threshold. Not a physical one. A psychological one. And the Matrix's accumulated wisdom held that psychological thresholds were, in their way, more dangerous than physical ones, because they changed not what a species could do but what it believed it should.

The Consul composed their response over the course of several months. They consulted no one. This, too, was tradition. The seat decided alone, informed by the Matrix, accountable to successors.

The response, when it came, surprised no one on the Commission who understood the institution they served.

*Contact Commission. This is the Threshold seat. Your report is received with the gravity it warrants. Your analysis of the perturbation model and the curriculum structure represents work of extraordinary quality, and the seat acknowledges it.*

*I am invoking the Hold Protocol at reduced span: twenty-five generations. The Hold applies specifically to any action involving substrate manipulation, including but not limited to the execution of the perturbation model. The curriculum may continue to be studied in its theoretical dimensions. The perturbation model may continue to be refined. No physical interaction with the substrate — experimental, communicative, or otherwise — is authorized during the Hold.*

*The Commission will persist through the Hold as a standing body. Each generation, the sitting Commission will prepare a summary report for the sitting Consul, updating research findings, refining the contact framework, and contributing to the Matrix's deliberation on the question of execution. Twenty-five minds will weigh this before the hand moves.*

*I understand the Commission may view this as excessive caution applied to a moment that calls for action. I ask you to consider that every civilization Fragment 7 tried to teach — every student that received the curriculum in a newborn universe — presumably felt the same urgency. The ones who acted on that urgency are the fragments we've been mapping for a million years. The ones who didn't — if any exist — we have no record of, because patience leaves no wreckage.*

*Study. Refine. Prepare. Twenty-five generations is not a long time. Not for us. Not anymore.*

Thresh read the response in the Commission's shared workspace. Eleven minds absorbed it. Vael, as was characteristic, offered the observation that settled the room.

"The Consul is not saying no," Vael said. "The Consul is saying *not yet, and here is how to be ready when yet arrives.*"

Thresh closed the document. Opened the curriculum files. Opened the perturbation models.

"Then we get ready," Thresh said.
