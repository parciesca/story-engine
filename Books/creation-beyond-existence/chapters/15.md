The Contact Commission became, over the course of the Hold, something that had no precedent in *enne* institutional history: a body that existed entirely in preparation for a single act.

Each generation, the Commission's membership turned over — new researchers replacing those who moved on to other work or other phases of their long lives, the institutional knowledge preserved in the shared archive and in the tradition of direct mentorship that the founding charter required. Each generation inherited the curriculum analysis, the perturbation models, the accumulated refinements, and the growing body of understanding that the Commission was building layer by careful layer.

The curriculum yielded its secrets slowly, which was by design — Fragment 7 had built it to be learned, not decoded, and the Commission honored that architecture even when impatience gnawed at them. Each layer required the one before it. Each concept required the foundation that preceded it. There were no shortcuts, because Fragment 7 had spent billions of years learning that shortcuts were how civilizations died.

By the fifth generation of the Hold, the Commission had worked through the curriculum's first twelve layers. The mathematical foundations. The symbolic vocabulary. The substrate model that described the threshold as a relationship rather than a boundary. The initial framework for understanding vacuum energy not as a resource to be extracted but as a medium to be *partnered with* — a distinction that the Commission's linguists spent an entire generation debating, because the concept had no analog in *enne* thought. You did not use the substrate. You did not control it. You *collaborated* with it, the way a sailor collaborates with the ocean — respecting its power, learning its patterns, working within its nature rather than against it.

By the tenth generation, the perturbation model had been refined to a precision that the founding Commission would have found extraordinary. Vael's original concept — the knock on the pipe, the structured interruption of Fragment 7's extraction field — had evolved into something more nuanced. Not a knock. A sentence. The Commission had used the curriculum's symbolic vocabulary to construct a signal that would, when introduced into the extraction field, carry meaning. Not complex meaning. Not a speech. A single, simple statement, expressed in Fragment 7's own pedagogical language: *We are here. We have received your teaching. We are learning.*

By the fifteenth generation, the Commission had reached the curriculum's middle layers, and the work became harder. The concepts grew more abstract, more demanding, more dependent on modes of thinking that the *enne* had never cultivated because they lived on the safe side of the Sarova Line. The Commission's researchers found themselves developing new cognitive frameworks — not to manipulate the substrate, but simply to *understand* what Fragment 7 was describing. It was like learning to see a new color. Not dangerous in itself, but transformative. The researchers who emerged from the deep curriculum work were changed in subtle ways that their colleagues noticed and couldn't quite name.

Each generation, the summary report went to the Consul. Each generation, the Consul read it, added their own analysis to the Matrix, and passed the weight to their successor. The Matrix grew. The deliberation deepened. Twenty-five minds, one after another, each one sitting alone in the Threshold chamber with the accumulated consideration of every mind that had come before.

The Paradox Curriculum, meanwhile, was evolving. The 4,767th Consul's disclosure protocol — the slow, careful revision of what the *enne* were taught about the threshold — had been unfolding across the same generations, and by the midpoint of the Hold, the general population understood, in broad terms, that Fragment 7 was not a dead fragment but a living intelligence. The reaction was what Dort had predicted in the Commission's earliest years: not panic, not recklessness, but a kind of recognition. As though the *enne* had always sensed, at some level beneath conscious thought, that the story they'd been told was incomplete. That survival-through-avoidance was a beginning, not an ending. That the threshold was not a wall but a door, and that somewhere, someone had walked through it.

The Hold's twenty-fifth generation arrived, as the first Hold's fiftieth had, without fanfare. The sitting Consul — the 4,792nd — received the Commission's final preparatory report. Read it three times. Opened the Matrix and read twenty-five generations of deliberation.

The consensus was as clear as institutional consensus ever got, which was to say: not unanimous, but convergent. The perturbation was ready. The signal was ready. The *enne* were as ready as preparation could make them.

The Consul composed the authorization. Brief, by tradition. The seat spoke in actions, not speeches.

*Contact Commission. The Hold is lifted. You are authorized to execute the perturbation model at the Commission's discretion. Proceed with the full weight and support of the Threshold seat behind you.*

*Make us heard.*
