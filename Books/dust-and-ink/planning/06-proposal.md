# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 06 — Before You Arrived
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — the watching-feeling becomes concrete. A rider was at the waystation before them, asking the right questions. Someone knew which road they were on — or cast a wide enough net. They have a half-day lead on a wrong road and one night to decide what to do with it.

## Beats
1. The road changing — farmland to drystone walls, older marker stones. A country that has shifted around them. Maret names the waystation ahead (Brenford, keeper named Gram) and says nothing else.
2. Arrival at Brenford. Gram: practical, experienced, charges four copper. A tinker's family in the corner. Davan's satchel against the wall.
3. Over porridge, Gram mentions it without emphasis: a rider came through this morning asking about a wagon out of Vardene, two aboard, merchant and a clerk.
4. Maret deflects cleanly: they're bound for Cressholm, market Thursday. Not Aldenmere. Gram accepts this and moves on.
5. Later, in the dark, Maret asks Gram what the rider looked like. Ordinary, dark coat, good horse. Seal ring on the right hand. Nothing else.
6. Davan works it out in the dark: the rider was ahead of them, not behind. Not a tail — a net. Either someone knows their route or there are several riders on several roads.
7. The seal ring. Not a guild mark he recognizes. May or may not match the doubled V from the commission paperwork.
8. Maret lays out the story: the manifest says what it says, nothing more. Cressholm is a half-day's wrong road bought them.
9. Davan says the ring could be nothing. Maret says yes. Neither of them believes it. One road north.

## POV
Close third Davan. Gram and Maret rendered through Davan's careful attention. The dark scene is interior — what he puts together without saying aloud.

## World established
- **Brenford waystation** — gray stone, efficient keeper named Gram, four copper for the floor, good water. A day and a half south of Aldenmere.
- **The rider** — dark coat, good horse, seal ring right hand. Was here before them. Asked the right questions. No name, no guild, no guild mark Davan can place.
- **Cressholm** — name Maret invents (or knows to be unused as a market town). The wrong road. Buys a half day.
- **The net** — either targeted route intelligence or a wide canvass. Both are alarming; one is worse.

## Notes
The chapter is quiet all the way through — the horror is in the architecture of the timing, not in any dramatic confrontation. The rider being ahead, not behind, is the thing. Maret's response is tactical and immediate; she doesn't panic, she lies and buys time. Davan is the one who lies awake doing the math.

Gram is not complicit — she's just a keeper who talks. The rider probably asked every waystation on every road out of Vardene. The fact that it works here is bad luck and a small road system.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

A rider was at Brenford waystation before them, asking about a merchant wagon from Vardene with two aboard. The rider had a seal ring on his right hand and a good horse and was going the right direction. Maret told Gram they were bound for Cressholm, not Aldenmere. They have a half day of wrong road bought them, maybe. The manifest says what it says. There is only one road north.

**A.** They push hard in the morning and try to reach Aldenmere by nightfall — arriving after dark, before the rider's net can be corrected. Maret knows a way in that doesn't go through the main gate. They get in, but arriving unannounced in the dark with altered paperwork is its own kind of risk.

**B.** On the road north, in open country past the last farms, something crosses the sky over the Vrethian Corridor — too far for shape but unmistakably large, unmistakably moving south. Not in the corridor. Sethverak has done something, or something has been done. The road to Aldenmere suddenly has a new urgency that has nothing to do with the rider.

**C.** Before the north fork, there is a roadside luck-shrine — a human one, small and practical, an old painted post with a clay dish at its feet. An old woman is there. She looks at Davan the way Perrin looked at him, which is to say: as if she already knew what she was seeing. She says something that is not a warning and is not comfort. The hedge-magic thread opens.
