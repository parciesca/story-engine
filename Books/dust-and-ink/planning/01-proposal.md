# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 01 — The Aldenmere Road
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Routine (opening chapter, setup)

## Beats
1. Morning on the road — Davan on the board, learning to sit wrong correctly. Establishes discomfort, Maret's competence, the horses.
2. The horses named Stub and Reliable — the joke and the grief behind the joke (her husband).
3. The defaced marker stone — Compact sigil scratched out. Maret reads it, says little, Davan doesn't have enough context to fully process it.
4. The dragon overhead, mid-morning, moving east-to-west. Out of corridor. Maret stops; Davan sees his first dragon. Brief, ominous, not dwelt on.
5. Sweetwater waystation at midday — the wool-trading family, the lone woman (elvish features, human road clothes). Maret: "don't stare."
6. Close: Davan's feeling that he's in something too large. The satchel at his feet.

## POV
Close third, primarily Davan. Maret holds the world-knowledge; Davan holds the reader's ignorance. His misreadings are the reader's access point.

## World established
- The Aldenmere road, Vardene origin, northern Midlands farmland
- The Threefold Accord signified on marker stones (interlocked rings)
- Dragon corridors exist — a dragon out of corridor is notable
- Maret's business, her horses, her husband Corren (dead four years)
- Davan's satchel habit — copies of everything
- Mael & Sons burned three days before story opens; Davan hired the same day

## Notes
Davan's satchel is introduced but not examined. The document is there; it doesn't announce itself. The chapter's work is establishing the texture of the world and the two of them together.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

The first day on the Aldenmere road has settled into its rhythms, with two things breaking the ordinary: a defaced marker stone and a dragon out of corridor. Neither has been fully processed. At the Sweetwater waystation, a lone woman with elvish features is eating alone.

**A.** Continue down the road — Ysse (the lone woman) catches up to them by late afternoon. She needs the same road; traveling in company is sensible; and she has a question about the toll gates at the Aldenmere border that she addresses to Davan specifically.

**B.** Davan, restless that evening at the waystation, opens his satchel to review his copies. He finds the document he transcribed three days before the fire — the one he marked only as *C.A. Addendum, per Mael's instruction* — and notices for the first time that several of the sigil marks in the header are not standard Compact notation. He can't read them. He doesn't know what they are.

**C.** They make camp for the night, and in the dark, something lands on a rock above the road. Not a dragon — smaller. A messenger-drake, the kind the Wyrm Courts use for short distances. It waits, patient and utterly still, with a sealed cylinder tied to its foreleg. It is looking at the wagon.
