# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 07 — Even After
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — the hedge-magic thread opens, quietly. A luck-reader at the shrine says the thing Davan has been carrying without naming it. Aldenmere is half a day north. Whatever the road is going to ask, it hasn't asked it yet.

## Beats
1. Morning departure from Brenford. Gram gives them porridge in a cup. No further conversation about the night. North into the border region — older stones, open hillside, fewer travelers.
2. The luck-shrine at the north fork: painted post, Compact and saint's mark, clay dish of offerings, the old woman on her stool.
3. Maret draws up without being asked. Pays for two cords.
4. The woman ties Maret's cord with road-blessing patter. Then ties Davan's — looks at his hands first, where they rest on the satchel strap.
5. While tying: "Scribe's hands. Ink stays in the lines, doesn't it. Even after." Said partly to herself. Not a warning, not comfort. Just the thing she noticed.
6. She finishes, presses the knot, tells them left fork for Aldenmere. That's all.
7. On the road: Davan turns the phrase over. The originals are ash. The copies are in the satchel. He does not think she knew — she just saw a scribe's hands and said what she saw. But she was accurate.
8. Maret: "She does that. Says the thing you're carrying. She's been at that fork for thirty years."
9. The newer marker stones here — replaced recently, within a few years. A sign of good administration or recent concern. Not noted aloud.
10. Half a day to Aldenmere. The cord on his wrist. Whatever the road asks, it hasn't asked yet.

## POV
Close third Davan. The luck-reader rendered through his practical observation — he resists the mystical reading while acknowledging she was accurate.

## World established
- **The luck-shrine at the north fork** — painted post, two marks, clay dish. The woman has been here thirty years. Maret knows her, or knows of her. This is a recognized type of person: someone who "says the thing."
- **Luck-reading as human small magic** — not dramatic, not reliable, but occasionally accurate. The woman looked at his hands and said what she saw. That is the whole of it — and it was enough.
- **The newer marker stones** — replaced recently on this stretch of road. Someone has been maintaining the northern approach with unusual care. Not commented on; noted.
- **Aldenmere: half a day north** — the journey's end is now visible.

## Notes
The hedge-magic thread opens here without declaring itself. Davan's rationalization ("she just saw a scribe's hands and said what she saw") is probably correct — and it is also accurate. The magic doesn't need to be more than that. The line "ink stays in the lines even after" does the thematic work for the chapter: the originals burned, the copies persist, what a scribe writes cannot be unwritten even when the physical document is gone. This applies to the Accord addendum as much as to Davan's hands.

Maret's "she does that" normalizes the luck-reader without dismissing her. This is how human small magic works: everyone knows someone like her, nobody takes it seriously, it is occasionally exactly right.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

They are half a day from Aldenmere. Davan has a knotted cord on his wrist and a phrase sitting in him that he doesn't know what to do with. Maret is watching the road. The marker stones on this stretch are newer than they should be. Whatever is waiting in Aldenmere has had time to prepare.

**A.** They arrive at Aldenmere. The gate is normal — paperwork, inspection, the usual — except that the gate-clerk asks one question that isn't on the standard form. Aldric Thresh's name is on the manifest counter-stamp. Something is off in how the clerk looks at it.

**B.** Before the gates, on the last stretch of road, they catch up to a traveler going the same direction: Ysse. Same road clothes, same unhurried pace. She doesn't seem surprised to see them. She falls into step beside the wagon.

**C.** They arrive and Maret goes to her usual contact for the delivery — a pottery buyer she's worked with for years. He's not at his shop. A neighbor says he left two days ago, no word. Maret's face does the thing it does when she's recalculating without showing it.
