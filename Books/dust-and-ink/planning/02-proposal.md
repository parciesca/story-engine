# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 02 — Four Scripts
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — the document's strangeness is confirmed to Davan; he now holds knowledge he does not understand and does not know who to trust with.

## Beats
1. Arrival at Whetham waystation. Hollis the keeper; floor-by-hearth quarters; Maret's practiced ease with the roads.
2. Supper outside. Maret packages the day's worries — the dragon, the scratched marker — into small efficient sentences. Davan recognizes the habit as a survival skill.
3. Maret sleeps. Davan takes Hollis's taper back to his corner and opens the satchel for his usual inventory.
4. For the first time since leaving Vardene he unties the bundle of copies. He does not know why tonight.
5. Memory: Mael himself delivered the C.A. Addendum three days before the fire. Stood over him until he agreed. Closed the office door.
6. Re-read as a reader. Body is unremarkable — formal Compact, committee prose. The work is in the header.
7. Four sigils. Two standard (Compact rings, Warden's Chancellor). The third and fourth are not any of the four scripts he knows — not Common, not Elvish formal, not Old Compact, not Merchant's Mark. Not even in the neighborhood.
8. He does not know what they are. He does not know who would.
9. He closes it. Lies down. Sleeps eventually.

## POV
Close third Davan, as Ch. 1. His professional eye is the instrument the reader uses to see the strangeness — his ignorance is the reader's ignorance.

## World established
- Whetham waystation (between Sweetwater and the next stop). Keeper: Hollis.
- Davan's four scripts: Common, Elvish formal, Old Compact, Merchant's Mark.
- The C.A. Addendum carries four sigils in its header. Two are standard Compact; two are unknown.
- Calendar reference: "Twelfth of Vreth" (the date Davan copied the Addendum).
- The attestation structure in the body echoes a Vardene military contract — small detail, available for later use.
- Mael's in-person handoff of the document is the first confirmed anomaly in the chain.

## Notes
Quiet chapter by design. No panic. Davan is not a decisive character; his strength is precise observation. The chapter's job is to raise the question, not answer it — the sigils are a door he now cannot un-open. The small sound in the stable at the end is ambient, not foreshadowing-heavy: keep ambiguity honest.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

Davan has opened his satchel for the first time since leaving Vardene and found, in the header of the C.A. Addendum, two sigils he cannot read and cannot place. They are professional, deliberate, not from any script he was trained in. He does not know what they are or who would know. Maret is asleep. The road continues in the morning.

**A.** In the morning, Davan shows the document to Maret. She isn't a scribe, but she's read papers all her life, and she may recognize *something*. He chooses plainness over caution.

**B.** He keeps the document to himself but asks Maret at breakfast, as casually as he can, whether she's ever heard of scripts outside the four he knows. Her answer will tell him something — but so will the question.

**C.** He tells no one. By the second hour on the road he has half-convinced himself he was tired and imagining things. Near noon they reach a crossroads, and the woman from Sweetwater — Ysse — is walking it, asking for a ride toward Aldenmere.
