# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 04 — Cups the Color of Tea
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — Davan asks the question out loud to a stranger. A second person now knows he is looking for a specific mark, and that person has reacted to it with a specific kind of pause.

## Beats
1. An hour from the Arden Crossroads, a flock of sheep crosses the road. A boy and his dog (named Bill). No-conflict encounter — pure country texture, ordinary life carrying on. Establishes breathing room after Ch. 3's tension.
2. The crossroads comes into view from a rise. Four roads, a broad paved circle, the Compact marker at the center intact. Davan notices whether markers are intact — a small tell of what he is now.
3. Under a willow at the southeast corner, a cider stand. The old man — **Perrin** — sits at a plank table with a brown jug and half a dozen cups the color of tea. Maret greets him by name. He knows her.
4. Maret buys apples for Reliable; Davan takes a cup. Perrin's hands are large on a small old body; his attention is quiet and total.
5. Davan, against the plan he had made on the walk to the plank, asks the question: has Perrin seen writing with nested arcs resolving to a glyph at the center?
6. Perrin's pause. The wind, the apples, Maret murmuring to Stub. Then, quietly: *"Where did you see that?"* Same voice as *"it's cider,"* different kind of question.
7. Chapter ends with Davan holding the cup, not yet answered. The floor of the room has shifted an inch without any sound.

## POV
Close third Davan. Perrin is rendered only through Davan's reading of him. The sheep encounter is the breath before the work.

## World established
- **Arden Crossroads** now on the page: broad paved circle, four roads, Compact marker intact at center, willow at the southeast corner.
- **Perrin** — old cider-stand keeper at Arden Crossroads. Knows Maret by name. Has a particular kind of quiet attention. Not frail. Hands large for his body.
- Country texture: hedge gates, shepherd boys, a working dog named Bill, a "boss sheep." The world continues around the main thread.
- Davan has begun to notice the condition of marker stones automatically — a small character development the narration registers.

## Notes
The sheep encounter is load-bearing in a non-obvious way: it lets the chapter arrive at Perrin without momentum from the prior scene. The quiet is the point. When Davan asks the question, he is genuinely breaking a self-imposed rule — he had decided on the walk from the wagon that he would not ask. The impulse itself is the turning.

Perrin's "Where did you see that?" in the same voice as "It's cider" is the chapter's hinge. He does not show surprise. He does not lean in. He simply asks, which is more alarming than any lean-in would have been. A man who does not need to adjust his voice to ask that question has answered another question already, which is: *he knows what Davan saw.*

The chapter ends on the question because that is the moment. The answer is the next chapter's work.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

Davan has asked a stranger — an old cider-stand keeper named Perrin, at the Arden Crossroads, who knows Maret by name — whether he has seen writing with nested arcs resolving to a glyph at the center. Perrin's answer was a question: *"Where did you see that?"* It arrived in the same flat tone he said *"It's cider"* in. He is waiting for an answer. Maret, ten feet away, has heard or has not heard. Davan has not answered yet.

**A.** Davan answers truthfully, as economically as he can: a document he copied. He does not say what kind of document; he does not say where. Perrin nods once and asks, just as quietly: *Who commissioned it?*

**B.** Davan deflects — he says he saw the mark in a grammar text at Mael & Sons, a fragment from somewhere, probably old, probably nothing. Perrin lets the lie sit. Then, as Davan is turning away with his cup, Perrin says, evenly: *Come back this way on your return. If you still want to know.*

**C.** Davan cannot find the words. The silence extends. Maret arrives at his elbow — she has heard; she does not interrupt. Perrin looks from Davan to Maret and back. After a moment he says: *Another time, then. Good roads to you both.* He pours Maret a cup and says nothing more.
