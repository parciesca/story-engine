# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 03 — Scribe's Curiosity
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — Davan commits to an action (the indirect question), and the question itself now exists in the record between him and Maret. His ignorance is no longer private.

## Beats
1. Dawn at Whetham. Davan slept badly; he has rehearsed four versions of the question between moonset and dawn. Decision carried in, nerves visible to him if not to Maret.
2. Wagon loaded. Breakfast on the bench — porridge, bread, tea.
3. The question, delivered as idle scribe's curiosity: is there writing beyond the four scripts he was trained in?
4. Maret's answer: *two*. Dragon script on sealed Warden-to-Warden dispatches (she has seen it twice at the Aldenmere gatehouse — nobody at the gate can read it). And something she has only heard of — an Arcanate-to-old-dragon-lineage script. No name. May not exist.
5. Maret's question: *"Why are you asking?"* — gentle, not suspicious, the same tone as everything else.
6. Davan's rehearsed lie: a nagging mark in a grammar text. Maret's nod is not belief; it is *filing*.
7. Back on the road. Maret re-enters the voice of the day — the cider stand at Arden Crossroads, Reliable's fondness for apples. Identical cadence to pre-breakfast, which Davan cannot read as kindness, normalcy, or both.
8. He watches her hands on the reins. Watches the empty sky. Realizes the absence of the dragon is as loud as the dragon had been.
9. Closing internal: the third sigil and the fourth sigil sat beside each other in the same row. Dragon script and Arcanate-dragon script. He has no word for what that means. Whatever he does next, he does without Maret's help.

## POV
Close third Davan. Maret is the object of his observation — she holds the knowledge; he probes. The reader reads her through him.

## World established
- **Dragon script** exists in the world, seen on sealed Warden-to-Warden dispatches. Rare. Unreadable at gate-level; Warden's own clerk may read it.
- **Arcanate-to-old-dragon-lineage script** — rumored, unnamed, possibly real. Heard on "old-road talk."
- **Arden Crossroads** — a stop on the road between Whetham and Aldenmere, with a cider stand run by an old man. Not yet visited.
- Davan's four trained scripts named for the record: Common, Elvish formal (read only), Old Compact, Merchant's Mark.
- Maret has worked the Aldenmere gatehouse vicinity long enough to have seen dragon script twice in four years.

## Notes
The chapter's turn is small and interior. Maret answers — generously, even — but her nod at the end is the chapter's hinge: she is now carrying his question as data, not as conversation. Davan reads this correctly and reads his own aloneness from it. No panic. No melodrama. Maret is not the enemy; she is simply not available to him for this.

The internal sequence at the close (third sigil + fourth sigil side by side = dragon + Arcanate-dragon, in the same document header) is the connection the reader can now make with Davan. He does not have a word for it because the text on the page of the Accord is *exactly* what that combination would be: a document signed between a human party and a dragon party, authenticated by the Arcanate, without elvish involvement. The reader who has the bible has the shape of this; Davan has only the shape of unease.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

Davan has asked. Maret has answered. She named two scripts beyond the four he knows — dragon script on sealed Warden-to-Warden dispatches, and a rumored Arcanate-to-old-dragon-lineage script. He hears, for the first time, a word for what may be in his satchel. Her question — *"Why are you asking?"* — arrived gentle and did not go away. His rehearsed answer was filed, not believed.

**A.** They reach the Arden Crossroads cider stand near midday. The old man who runs it has watched travelers at this crossroads longer than anyone. When Davan pays for a cup, the old man holds his eye a beat too long. Davan — not quite meaning to — tests him: *have you ever seen writing with nested arcs that resolve to a glyph at the center?* The old man looks at him, and after a pause says: *where did you see that?*

**B.** Davan says nothing more all day. The afternoon passes in silence. By evening they are at the next waystation, and a courier arrives with the white-feather pin of a Warden's dispatch rider. He takes a room. At dinner, he is civil. He does not speak to Maret or Davan. But he is watching.

**C.** In the late afternoon, the weather breaks — a hard summer storm rolls in fast, rain and wind, and they take shelter at a roadside shrine. The small stone box of the shrine is dry and narrow. Inside, on the wall, someone has recently carved a small mark. It is not the Compact's sigil. It is not any script Davan knows. It matches the third sigil from the Addendum.
