# CHAPTER PROPOSAL

**Chapter:** 05 — The Copy
**Branch:** main
**Turning point level:** Significant — Davan tells Maret everything. The dam breaks. Both of them now carry what he's been carrying since the fire.

## Beats
1. Close of the Perrin scene (Ch. 4 Choice C): silence extends, Maret arrives, Perrin reads the room and closes with hospitality and precise economy. "Another time, then. Good roads to you both." He pours Maret a cup without asking; she drinks and hands it back. The door is not locked — it's left ajar.
2. On the road. Maret names the long silence, not as a question. Davan has his satchel in his lap — moved there this morning without deciding to.
3. The disclosure: he keeps copies of everything he's ever transcribed. It's professional habit. He has a copy of the document with the nested arcs. In his satchel. Now. The "grammar text" was a lie.
4. Who commissioned it: Aldric Thorne, merchant, Vardene, Grain Lane — dead three years. A proxy name. Paperwork drawn up in a dead man's name, with an unregistered seal (doubled V inside a circle), assigned specifically to Davan by Mael himself.
5. Other work from the same seal: a land survey near the southern end of the Vrethian Corridor (Davan copied it, thought nothing of it); a second piece Davan didn't copy — Benne Farr took it when Davan was ill. Benne died in the fire.
6. The fire: Davan left early that night. The archive burned. Five years of originals. The copy in his satchel is what's left.
7. Maret's observation: either they don't know about the copies, or they're watching where he goes. Said evenly. Both are on the table.
8. Road continues: marker stone, Reliable sighing, nothing resolved.

## POV
Close third Davan. Maret rendered through his reading of her — her silences as precise as her words.

## World established
- **Aldric Thorne** — dead merchant of Vardene, Grain Lane. Used as a proxy name; his counting house folded autumn before last. Identity of the actual client unknown.
- **The client seal** — doubled V inside a circle, unregistered with any guild.
- **Benne Farr** — scribe at Mael & Sons who copied the second piece from the same client. Died in the fire. Establishes that the fire was targeted at people with knowledge of the commission.
- **Land survey near the Vrethian Corridor's southern terminus** — part of the same commission chain. Significance unknown.
- **Davan's copying habit** — now explicit: locked chest in his room above the house, copies of everything. The chest survived because he had already left.

## Notes
The Perrin close is short by design — the chapter's weight is on the road. Perrin's "another time" is not a dismissal; it is a different kind of invitation, and Davan knows it. But that's a different chapter.

The disclosure scene works because Maret already knew part of it. She filed his lie in Ch. 3; this chapter is the ledger coming due. Her "I know" is the most important line — it confirms she saw through him and said nothing, which means she has been waiting, not pressuring. That is her greatest courtesy.

The Benne detail is the chapter's hinge. Davan has been carrying guilt, not just danger.

# CHAPTER HANDOFF

Maret knows everything now. The document in Davan's satchel is a copy of a commission placed by an unknown party using a dead man's name, with two sigils in the header that match what Maret described — dragon script, Arcanate-to-lineage script — and a third sigil Perrin recognized but did not name. Benne Farr, who copied the second piece for the same client, died in the fire. The originals are ash. The copy is in a satchel on a merchant wagon going north on the Aldenmere road.

**A.** Maret turns practical — she's a merchant; she thinks in routes and risks. She doesn't say they should turn back; turning back might be worse. But she has thoughts about what they declare at the Aldenmere toll gate, who they speak to or avoid, and whether their cargo manifest needs to look different. She begins to plan aloud. Davan listens.

**B.** No plan yet — the road makes the decision for them. That night at the waystation, or the next day, something signals they may not be as unnoticed as they hoped: a courier moving the wrong direction, riders who take too long to pass, a waystation keeper who asks an odd question. The watching-feeling becomes concrete.

**C.** Maret asks to see it. Not the whole document — she can't read the sigils — but she wants to hold what this is. Davan opens the satchel. She takes the copy, looks at the header marks, turns it once in her hands. Sets it back on the bench between them without comment. Drives.
