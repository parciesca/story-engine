STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Cold War spy thriller
  Tone: Slow-burn, paranoid, literary — le Carré register. Tension through inference and implication, not action. Institutional paranoia. Moral ambiguity without resolution.
  Core premise: In Helsinki, autumn 1972, British SIS Head of Station Elias Vane receives a walk-in claiming a 15-year Soviet asset sits inside Finland's foreign ministry — timed perfectly to poison the approaching CSCE talks. But the walk-in knew a classified contact protocol, and someone is already watching Elias watch the walk-in. The question of whether the intelligence is genuine, a provocation, or something stranger will unspool across the story.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Elias Vane — British SIS Head of Station, Helsinki. Cover: First Secretary (Commercial), British Embassy. Age 49. Former classicist (Oxford). Recruited late 1940s. Has spent his career as an analyst and assessor — a reader of people, not a field officer. Gift for languages: Finnish, Russian, German. Speaks them with a slight delay, as if translating in his head; people underestimate him for it. Precise, still, socially graceful at a distance. His professional gift and social liability are the same thing: he can be very, very quiet.
    Voice anchor: Asks questions by not asking them — silence and posture as technique. Rarely commits to a conclusion in company. "Tell me" is his preferred opener.
    Status: active

  Viktor Gromov — Soviet Trade Representative, Helsinki Residency. Age ~50. Six years in Helsinki posting, two prior postings. Wife and daughter (age 17) in Leningrad. Compact build, grey Soviet-cut suit. Walk-in: claims knowledge of a 15-year-deep Soviet asset inside the Finnish foreign ministry, active ahead of CSCE preliminary talks. Passed Elias a summary document containing a name. Knew SIS Helsinki Station's internal contact protocol — provenance unexplained: claims a dead friend from Leningrad taught him.
    Voice anchor: Speaks in the abstract before reaching substance. Does not call himself a patriot. Uses words like "proof" with careful deliberateness.
    Status: active (relationship to service unclear — defector? dangle? something else?)

  The Watcher — Unnamed woman, observed on Esplanadi bench as Elias departed the café. Read a newspaper cover-to-front — trained surveillance behavior. Identity unknown. Not yet in file.
    Status: active (unknown affiliation)

  Seija Mäkinen — Finnish intelligence (SUPO), Elias's liaison contact. Age ~38. Pragmatic; deeply conversant with Finland's geopolitical tightrope act. Has not yet appeared on page.
    Status: active (introduced in bible only)

  Pamela Secker — Elias's controller, London. Complicated prior history (nature unspecified). Has not yet appeared on page.
    Status: active (introduced in bible only)

WORLD RULES
  - Setting: Helsinki, Finland, October 1972.
  - Finland's geopolitical position: officially neutral, subject to significant Soviet pressure (Finlandization). Must balance diplomatic relationship with Moscow against Western alignment. Finnish intelligence (SUPO) operates in this constraint.
  - The CSCE (Conference on Security and Co-operation in Europe) preliminary talks are approaching. Helsinki has been designated host. Every intelligence service in Europe will be swarming in — Soviet, American, British, French, West and East German, and the smaller services of the satellite states.
  - SIS contact protocols are distributed on limited circulation — station-level, with named recipients. The Helsinki Station protocol was distributed to Helsinki, Stockholm, and Oslo in June 1972.
  - Intelligence that arrives pre-packaged with personal resonance (a name the recipient will recognize) is, in Elias's experience, a red flag. Good intelligence is rarely flattering to its recipient.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - The texture of rehearsed truth vs. genuine truth — the unreliability of sincerity as a test
  - Institutional loyalty and its costs (the question of where Elias's loyalty ultimately sits)
  - Finland as metaphor: the neutral country that survives by watching carefully and saying little

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - Is Gromov genuine, a provocation (dangle), or something in between?
  - How did Gromov know the SIS Helsinki contact protocol?
  - Who is the watcher on Esplanadi, and who sent her?
  - What name is in the envelope, and what is Elias's history with them?
  - Is there actually a Soviet asset inside the Finnish foreign ministry?

TIMELINE & STATE
