STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Mystery / hardboiled noir.
  Tone: Dry, melancholic, quietly observant. Walt's voice is wry and economical; the prose carries the weight of a city in slow decline.
  Core premise: Spring 1974, Detroit. Walter Reardon — ex-Detroit PD, three years out, working out of a one-room office above a Polish lunch counter on Cass Avenue — is hired by Loretta Mills to find her brother Marcus, an autoworker who has been missing two weeks and whose disappearance the police have made it their business not to solve.
  Prose discipline (author note): Avoid "the way a [person] [verbs]" and "in a [adj] way" constructions — they read as crutch phrasing. Reach for the concrete detail or the direct sentence instead.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Walter "Walt" Reardon — Mid-40s, ex-Detroit PD detective. Three years out of the department under circumstances not yet revealed. Now a private investigator on Cass Avenue. Wry, apathetic about the city's larger rot, but focused once a case has hooks in him. Drinks his coffee, doesn't volunteer information about himself. Voice anchor: economical, observational — "He had given up, by that spring, on a number of things." Status: active.

  Loretta Mills — Twenty-five or twenty-six. Dignified, careful, articulate. Unmarried, raising a young daughter. Has come to Walt because someone she trusts said he used to be honest. Voice anchor: precise and measured, no theatrics — "I thanked him for his time." Status: active.

  Marcus Mills (off-page, missing) — Twenty-eight. Loretta's brother. Trim-line worker at Dodge Main in Hamtramck. Two-tour Army vet (Fort Hood, returned 1969, doesn't talk about it). Reads. Was joining the DRUM caucus at the plant. Per Loretta, the kind of man who doesn't miss appointments — had a stuffed giraffe in a paper bag for a Belle Isle zoo trip with his niece, never showed. Status: missing two weeks.

  Tasha Mills (off-page) — Loretta's young daughter, around eight. Was supposed to go to the zoo with Uncle Marcus. Status: active.

  Tom Wilcox (off-page, named) — Foreman at Dodge Main. Marcus's foreman. Loretta says he had it in for Marcus. Status: unmet, active.

  DRUM — Dodge Revolutionary Union Movement. Black autoworkers' caucus active at Dodge Main. Marcus had joined and was, per Loretta, in the middle of "words he wouldn't repeat in front of her."

  [Names mentioned in planning but not yet in prose: Stan Kovac (potential ex-DPD partner), Eddie Gillis (potential UAW steward). To be confirmed by the chapter the user steers toward.]

WORLD RULES
  - Setting: Detroit, mid-April 1974. Coleman Young is in his first months as mayor; he has just told the suburbs that the criminals would have to keep moving. The city is in slow decline; the auto industry is straining; the oil crisis has lingered. The Renaissance Center is under construction along the river.
  - Walt's office is above a Polish lunch counter on Cass Avenue, in the Cass Corridor — the most derelict zone in the city in this era. He has a steel desk, a green metal filing cabinet, and a calendar from a Hamtramck body shop still turned to February.
  - The Detroit Police Department is treated from a distance. Walt was part of it once; he isn't anymore. The relationship is unresolved and unfriendly.
  - Dodge Main in Hamtramck is operating in 1974 (will close in 1980, outside the story's likely frame). Trim-line work, line-speed politics, race-and-labor tension on the floor.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - A city declining slowly enough that the people inside it can pretend not to notice.
  - The cost of having been honest, once.
  - Race, labor, and who gets looked for when they don't come home.
  - Apathy as armor — and what gets through it.

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - Where is Marcus Mills? Alive, dead, run, taken?
  - What was Marcus mixed up in at the plant — labor, drugs, something else?
  - Why won't the precinct find Loretta's missing-persons report?
  - What got Walt off the force three years ago?
  - Whom did Walt call at the end of Chapter 1?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Walt's office, Cass Avenue, Detroit.
  Time position: Thursday morning, mid-April 1974.
  Key events so far:
    - Ch 1: Loretta Mills hires Walt to find her brother Marcus, missing two weeks. Walt takes two of four twenties she puts on the desk, agrees to three days. As she leaves, he picks up the phone and dials a number he has not dialed in three years.

READER PROMISES
  - The case will resolve, one way or another. Walt will find out what happened to Marcus.
  - Walt's exit from the DPD will come into focus over time.
  - The precinct's "lost" report points to corruption or suppression — that thread pays off.
  - The DRUM caucus and Tom Wilcox will be revisited.
  - The stuffed giraffe in the paper bag is the kind of detail that earns its place — its weight will land later.
