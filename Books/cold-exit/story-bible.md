STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: sci-fi space adventure (first person)
  Tone: tense, claustrophobic, wonder-threaded dread — lone human against an enormous silence; the horror is scale, not gore
  Core premise: Keziah Dhar, a freelance deep-space surveyor, detects an impossible mass anomaly buried inside a carbonaceous asteroid in the Kepler-442 outer belt. What she finds is not a geological oddity — it is a vessel, or something like one, built to a scale no human civilization has attempted, clearly alien, and still powered. Her comm relay is days out of range. She is alone, and it already knows she is there.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Keziah "Kez" Dhar — Protagonist, narrator. 34. Former Imperial Navy EOD specialist; left after the Vashti Station incident (details withheld, self-withheld). Now a licensed independent surveyor operating under a contract with Kepler Mineral Authority. Pragmatic, sardonic, careful. She narrates in the present-tense immediacy of someone trying to hold herself together by cataloguing facts. Voice anchor: dry, precise, occasionally startled into honesty by her own feelings.
    Status: active
  Tomasz Wierzbicki — Kez's former survey partner. Died of sudden cardiac arrest 11 months ago, alone in the bunk beside the engine room. Not dramatic — just stopped. Kez has not filed for a replacement crew member. The empty bunk has a jacket hanging on it.
    Status: deceased (referenced, off-page)
  The Yevgenia — Kez's ship. A Cutter-class converted survey vessel, 22 meters long. Cramped, reliable, smells like ozone and old coffee. Named by Tomasz after his grandmother; Kez kept the name. Sensor suite, a drilling probe (the "Ferret"), two emergency suits, standard comms rig.
    Status: active (vessel)
  The Object — Designation: Anomaly 4K-7741-A. An artificial structure approximately 800 meters along its principal axis, buried 2.7km under the surface of asteroid 4-Kep-7741. Material composition unknown — apparent density 38,000 tonnes/m³, which is physically implausible for any known matter state at this temperature and pressure. It is powered. Surface is a deep-water color, absorbs light across unusual spectral ranges, bears deliberate ridged geometry and a visible seam (probable hatch) on its principal face. The rock immediately surrounding it (~400m radius) has been altered — fused, structured — likely by long-term proximity effects. It has the capability to briefly take control of external objects (demonstrated: Ferret redirected 14 degrees during a 3.7-second systems blackout). The redirect was purposeful: the Ferret was aimed at the seam.
    Status: active (entity of unknown nature; demonstrated ability to interact with proximate objects)

WORLD RULES
  - Setting: 2387. The Kepler-442 system, colonized ~60 years ago. Two habitable moons (Keshet and Varro), heavy industrial presence. The outer belt is sparsely surveyed — contract work, mostly solo.
  - FTL: does not exist. Transit between systems takes decades via cryo-generation ships. Kepler-442 is effectively isolated — communication with Sol takes 3 years one-way.
  - Communications: in-system relay buoys; Kez's nearest relay buoy is 4.2 days at standard burn. Real-time comms exist up to about 200,000km. Beyond that: burst-packet transmissions to relay nodes.
  - Surveyor culture: independent contractors, licensed by planetary authority, discoverer's rights enforced by treaty. Finding something this significant legally belongs to whoever files the claim first.
  - The Ferret: Kez's drilling probe — a 40cm autonomous unit that can drill up to 4km into soft-to-medium rock, carrying a sensor suite. Normally used for core sampling. Can be piloted remotely.
  - EOD background: Kez has a practiced calm around things that might kill her. She approaches danger the way she approaches a live charge — methodically, without rushing, with full awareness that hurrying is how you die.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Scale as dread: the Object is enormous; Kez is tiny. The story is interested in what a person does when the universe presents something categorically beyond human scale.
  - Competence as coping: Kez manages fear through procedure. This works until it doesn't.
  - The cost of being alone: Tomasz's absence is felt in the gaps — things Kez would have said to him, decisions she'd have made differently. Grief as background radiation.
  - Discoverer's rights vs. discoverer's survival: the legal-economic pressure to claim the find creates tension against the instinct to run.

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - What is the Object? What is its purpose? Who built it?
  - Why is it still powered after — how long? Thousands of years? The altered rock suggests a long residence.
  - The redirect: the Object aimed the Ferret at its own seam/hatch. An invitation? A test? Something else?
  - Does Kez report this before investigating? The closer she gets, the more she risks; the further she waits, the more another surveyor might stumble onto it.
  - What is behind the seam? The Object knows where it wants her to look.
  - What happened at Vashti Station? (Background tension, Kez's history — not yet surfaced fully)

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: asteroid 4-Kep-7741, Kepler-442 outer belt — approximately 4.2 days from the nearest relay buoy at standard burn
  Inventory/resources: Yevgenia fully fueled; 38 days of consumables; the Ferret (drilling probe, fully charged); two EVA suits; standard armament (one emergency flare pistol, not useful)
  Time position: Day 19 of a 60-day survey contract. Sol date approximately 2387.08.14.
  Key events so far:
    Ch.01 — Kez's routine mass scan of 4-Kep-7741 returns impossible density readings from a 2.7km depth. She runs diagnostics, confirms the anomaly is real, and identifies the approximate dimensions of the buried structure (800m axis). As she prepares the Ferret for a closer scan, the Object emits a single seismic pulse through the rock — not natural, not random. It felt like an acknowledgment.
    Ch.02 — Kez deploys the Ferret and descends through 2.7km of rock. The final 400m show altered, fused geology — proximity effects. She reaches the Object's exterior and confirms the anomaly visually: non-natural geometry, unusual spectral signature, a visible seam on the surface. During a 3.7-second blackout of all Ferret systems, the Object redirects the probe 14 degrees to aim it directly at the seam. Systems restore nominal. The Ferret floats 11 meters from the Object's surface, pointed at what might be a door.

READER PROMISES
  - The Object will be more than a geological mystery — it will confront Kez directly
  - Tomasz's death and Vashti Station are threads that will weave into who Kez is under pressure
  - The discoverer's rights framework will create a real conflict (Kez vs. the system, or Kez vs. another claimant)
  - Whatever is inside the Object, encountering it will cost Kez something
