STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Contemporary fiction
  Tone: Warm, wry, observational humor. Gentle irreverence — the comedy lives in human
    behavior, the gap between how things should work and how they actually work. Twain's
    eye for the absurd in the ordinary, Osman's affection for his characters. Witty but
    never mean. These are competent people in a place that mostly runs itself, until it doesn't.
  Core premise: The daily life and quiet dramas of a small-town city hall — the handful
    of people who keep the machinery of local government turning, and what happens when
    something disrupts the rhythm.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Janet Roby — Front desk, 23-year veteran. Arrives at 7:40, reads the Courier,
    takes pity on visitors defeated by the stuck door. Warm, steady, the building's
    first point of contact. Voice anchor: dry economy — "You're a good man, Marcus Webb."
    Status: active
  Dale Kessler — Town manager, 11 years. Travel mug, WORLD'S OKAYEST DAD coaster
    from his daughter. Handles things like drainage easements and Phil Henderson with
    patient persistence. Status: active
  Bev Castillo — Permits, zoning, code enforcement. Considers 8:10 early. Her approach
    to Art Pollard's illegal boat is her philosophy of governance: rules exist, but so
    do people. Status: active
  Marcus Webb — Public works, age 28, 3 years in. Took the job because it called back,
    stayed because Millhaven became his. Accidental Tuesday donut tradition he can't quit.
    Status: active
  Elena Marsh — Town clerk, drops her son at school at 7:55, arrives at 8:15 without
    variation. Institutional memory no filing system can replicate. Knows when every pipe
    was last replaced and finds suspiciously low bids suspicious. Status: active
  Roger — Maintenance contractor. Mondays and Thursdays, back permitting. Hasn't fixed
    the left front door since 2019. Status: active (peripheral)
  Linda — Accounting, remote 3 days/week. Appears in person for meetings and the color
    printer. Status: active (peripheral)

WORLD RULES
  - Millhaven, population ~4,200. Small American town, modern day.
  - Municipal Building has limestone columns and a left front door that sticks.
  - Full-time city hall staff: five. Plus Roger (contractor) and Linda (remote accounting).
  - Local politics only — no national/geopolitical threads.
  - The Millhaven Courier publishes twice weekly and takes about four minutes to read.
  - Art Pollard has an illegal boat on his front lawn. His wife died two years ago.
    The boat was their last purchase together. Bev calls periodically. The boat stays.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Community: the building as a place that belongs to its people
  - Dignity of small work: drainage easements, fence permits, donut traditions —
    the machinery of a town, treated with quiet respect
  - Staying: Marcus stayed because the town became his. These people are here by choice.
