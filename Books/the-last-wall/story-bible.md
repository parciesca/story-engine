STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Adventure / Historical Fiction
  Tone: Visceral, grounded, morally complex. The texture of a dying world — not elegiac,
    but immediate. Every decision costs something. Violence is not clean. Loyalty is not
    simple. The story earns its tragedy by first earning its life.
  Core premise: Constantinople, April–May 1453. The last Christian city of the East is
    besieged by the Ottoman army of Mehmed II. Aleksios Doukanas — Genoese father,
    Byzantine mother, fluent in three languages and loyal to no flag — serves as
    interpreter between the desperate defenders. As the walls crack, so does every
    allegiance he thought he held.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Aleksios Doukanas — Interpreter, 23. Genoese father, Byzantine mother (minor nobility).
    Speaks Greek, Italian, Turkish, some Arabic. Works between the Imperial court and
    the Genoese contingent. Voice anchor: dry, observant, says less than he knows.
    Watches powerful men from just outside the circle of power. Status: active.

  Paolo Doukanas — Aleksios's father, 54. Genoese merchant, thirty years in
    Constantinople, successful by reading situations early. Has three passages booked on
    a Venetian galley. Voice anchor: methodical sentences, never raises his voice, always
    counting. Status: active.

  Giovanni Giustiniani Longo — Genoese commander, 38. Brought 700 professional soldiers
    at his own expense. Brilliant tactician, exhausted, running on competence and
    stubbornness. Voice anchor: questions, not commands — he gets things done by forcing
    other people to think. Status: active.

  Constantine XI Palaiologos — The last Byzantine Emperor, 58. Dignified, alone, fully
    aware of what is coming. Has sent for help from every Christian court in Europe;
    none came. Voice anchor: formal, precise, never performs hope he doesn't feel.
    Status: active.

  Eleni Doukainas — Aleksios's mother, 49. Minor Byzantine noble family, the Doukas
    branch reduced to one house and a name. Refuses to leave. Voice anchor: quiet,
    certain, unanswerable. Status: active (not yet on-page).

WORLD RULES
  - Constantinople, April 1453: city population ~50,000, defenders ~7,000 (including
    ~2,000 Genoese/Venetian professionals). Ottoman force: ~80,000 with naval support.
  - The Theodosian Walls: triple-layered land walls, moat + outer wall + inner wall,
    built 5th century. Have never been breached by external assault. Urban's bombard
    is the first weapon capable of threatening them.
  - Urban's cannon: cast by Urban of Transylvania (Hungarian), 27 feet long, fires
    ~1200-lb stone balls. Reload time ~3 hours. The sound alone causes physical damage.
  - The Golden Horn: the harbor inlet on the city's north side, blocked by a great iron
    chain to prevent Ottoman ships entering. The Genoese colony of Galata sits on the
    opposite shore — nominally neutral, practically sympathetic to the defenders.
  - Giustiniani commands the critical Blachernae section (northernmost, single-walled,
    the tactical weak point). The Emperor walks the walls daily.
  - Aleksios's access: he can move through the city freely, enter the Blachernae palace,
    and cross to Galata by boat. He has limited but real access to Ottoman-side
    information via the Galata Genoese, who trade with both sides.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - The question of belonging: Aleksios is legible to everyone and native to no one.
    Translators live in the gap between worlds; the gap is closing.
  - What survives: the walls can be rebuilt; the people behind them cannot. What does
    it mean to save yourself when the thing you were part of cannot be saved?
  - Competence under impossible conditions: Giustiniani, the Emperor, Aleksios — all
    doing the best possible thing in a situation where the best possible thing may not
    be enough.

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - Paolo's three passages: Aleksios has until tomorrow night to decide. Mother won't go.
    This is a live wire.
  - What Giustiniani actually thinks: he's too good a soldier not to know the math.
    Does he believe they can hold, or is he buying time for something else?
  - The Ottoman side: we've seen the cannon but not the people firing it. Mehmed II is
    21 years old and obsessed with this siege. That story is unwritten.

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Constantinople, land walls (Blachernae section) and surrounds
  Time position: April 6, 1453 — Day 1 of the artillery bombardment. Siege began
    April 2. The city will fall May 29. We are 53 days from the end.
  Inventory/resources: Aleksios has freedom of movement, father's money (for now),
    three languages, and no weapon beyond a belt knife.
  Key events so far:
    Ch 1 — Urban's bombard fires its first shot; outer wall breached, immediately
      repaired. Aleksios runs the Emperor's message to the Venetian bailo. Father
      offers three passages out. Aleksios watches the Emperor walk the walls at dusk.

READER PROMISES
  - The father's escape offer will come due. Three passages, one night, one choice.
  - The Emperor walking the walls alone — we owe that image a payoff.
  - Aleksios's Turkish: he speaks the enemy's language. That will matter.
  - The mother, Eleni, has been named but not shown. She carries something.
  - Giustiniani's pragmatism vs. the Emperor's dignity — these two men will collide.
