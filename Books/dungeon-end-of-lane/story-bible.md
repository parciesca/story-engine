STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Light fantasy adventure / classic CYOA
  Tone: Warm, whimsical, gently humorous — the register of a classic
    Choose-Your-Own-Adventure paperback, affectionate toward the tropes
    it plays with. Stakes are real but nobody needs to be grim about it.
  Core premise: You live in the village of Millhaven, which has had a
    dungeon at the end of Cobble Lane for three centuries. You've walked
    past it your whole life. Today — for reasons you can't entirely
    explain — you're actually going in. Properly.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  YOU — The protagonist. A capable-enough, sensible-enough local who has
    finally decided that "person who never explored the famous dungeon on
    their street" is not a satisfying legacy. Aged twenty-six. Owns a
    good belt knife (gift from an aunt), one length of rope, second-best
    boots. Not reckless; not a coward. Somewhere in the useful middle.
    Voice: Wry internal commentary, practical instincts, occasional
    excessive optimism.

  Old Marta — Elderly vendor stationed just inside the dungeon entrance.
    Has sold "adventuring supplies" at this spot for as long as anyone
    can remember. Her supplies are of debatable quality; her assessments
    of adventurers are uncomfortably accurate. Speaks in short sentences.
    Knows more than she lets on. Voice: Blunt, dry, slightly oracular.
    Status: active (entrance only)

WORLD RULES
  - Millhaven is a village that has coexisted with its dungeon for three
    hundred years. The dungeon is a local institution, not a source of
    terror.
  - The first two chambers of the main path serve as the baker's root
    cellar. This is considered perfectly normal.
  - The dungeon interior is lit by bioluminescent blue-green lichen
    ("glowmoss"), which grows throughout. Candles are optional, not
    required.
  - Three passages branch immediately from the entrance: the fine-carved
    left arch, the rougher right tunnel, and the broad main path ahead.
  - The Fletcher twins went left seven years ago and came back rich but
    won't discuss it. Orvyn Fletcher goes pale at the word "obligation."
  - Bastwick allegedly found a lucky mushroom in the right tunnel that
    let him speak to fish for a week.
  - Marta's hint: "The left one's faster" — faster to what, unknown.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Familiarity as the enemy of adventure: the dungeon has always been
    there; the hard part was deciding to go.
  - Practicality versus wonder: you've packed sensibly, but the dungeon
    will require more than rope and a good knife.
  - The gap between reputation and reality (each path has a legend;
    what's actually there may be different).

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - What happened to the Fletcher twins in the left passage? What is
    the "obligation"?
  - What is Marta's actual relationship to the dungeon?
  - What does "faster" mean, in Marta's cryptic hint?
  - What awaits on each of the three paths?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Entrance to Brackenmoor Delve, Millhaven
  Inventory: belt knife, rope (coiled), dried meat, full waterskin,
    second-best boots
  Time position: Morning, day one
  Active branch: main
  Key events so far:
    Ch 1: Arrived at dungeon entrance. Met Marta. Declined her wares
      (already had rope). Faced the three-way split.

READER PROMISES
  - Each path will be genuinely different in tone and content
  - Marta's cryptic comment about the left arch will pay off
  - The Fletcher twins' "obligation" will be explained if that path
    is taken
  - The story will maintain its light register even when things go
    sideways — genre-consistent humor throughout
