STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Light fantasy adventure / classic CYOA
  Tone: Warm, whimsical, gently humorous — the register of a classic
    Choose-Your-Own-Adventure paperback, affectionate toward the tropes
    it plays with. Prose has a slight lyrical jauntiness: more playful
    rhythms, sentences that swing a little, comic details given their due
    weight. The character is being lulled into confidence. Not Tom
    Bombadil — more like a particularly charming travel narrator who
    hasn't realised the landscape is about to become interesting.
  Core premise: You live in the village of Millhaven, which has had a
    dungeon at the end of Cobble Lane for three centuries. You've walked
    past it your whole life. Today — for reasons you can't entirely
    explain — you're actually going in. Properly. Via the normal route.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  YOU — The protagonist. A capable-enough, sensible-enough local who has
    finally decided that "person who never explored the famous dungeon on
    their street" is not a satisfying legacy. Aged twenty-six. Owns a
    good belt knife (gift from an aunt), one length of rope, dried meat,
    full waterskin, second-best boots. Not reckless; not a coward.
    Somewhere in the useful middle. Reads ledgers. Respects notice boards.
    Voice: Wry internal commentary, practical instincts, building
    confidence that is probably slightly misplaced.

  Old Marta — Elderly vendor stationed just inside the dungeon entrance.
    Has sold "adventuring supplies" at this spot for as long as anyone
    can remember. Her supplies are of debatable quality; her assessments
    of adventurers are uncomfortably accurate. Speaks in short sentences.
    Knows more than she lets on. Voice: Blunt, dry, slightly oracular.
    Status: active (entrance only)

  Unknown Notice Board Correspondent(s) — Two parties, at minimum, have
    been maintaining and annotating the notice board in the hexagonal
    antechamber. The small, tidy hand writes official-style notices. The
    large, bolder hand asks questions. Their relationship and nature are
    unknown. Status: unknown, likely active

WORLD RULES
  - Millhaven is a village that has coexisted with its dungeon for three
    hundred years. The dungeon is a local institution, not a source of terror.
  - The first two chambers of the main path serve as the baker's root
    cellar (flour, root vegetables, shelving, a good stool). Normal.
  - The dungeon interior is lit by bioluminescent blue-green glowmoss,
    which grows throughout and thickens in the deeper sections.
  - Three passages branch from the entrance corridor; they converge in
    the hexagonal antechamber (vaulted ceiling, spiral glowmoss, four exits).
  - The hexagonal antechamber has a maintained notice board: map of the
    first chambers, a DO NOT FEED THE ECHOES notice, an annotated dialogue.
  - Echoes: living residents of the gallery beyond the antechamber.
    They appear as shimmering, vaguely architectural displacements in the
    air — roughly cat-sized, no fixed shape. They collect sounds and
    replay them with evident satisfaction. "Feeding" them means giving
    them interesting audio to repeat; they grow brighter and more
    numerous when fed. Do not hum. "Right then" was fed to them
    inadvertently. The protagonist now has an Echo entourage of ~12,
    who are playing back footsteps and deliberate nose-breathing.
  - There is a knee-height gap in the antechamber wall, rounded edges,
    apparently made with care. Something rustled inside it.
  - The baker's ledger recorded "the green smell getting stronger" in
    the second chamber. Marta said not to worry. This has not been resolved.
  - The Fletcher twins went left (from the entrance split) seven years
    ago and came back rich; Orvyn Fletcher goes pale at the word "obligation."
  - Bastwick allegedly found a lucky mushroom in the right tunnel that
    let him speak to fish for a week.
  - Marta's hint: "The left one's faster" — faster to what, unknown.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Familiarity as the enemy of adventure: the dungeon has always been
    there; the hard part was deciding to go.
  - Practicality versus wonder: you've packed sensibly, but the dungeon
    has its own logic.
  - Confidence as a pleasant trap: things are going well; things always
    seem to go well, just before they stop going quite as planned.
  - The dungeon as a managed space: someone maintains this. It has rules.
    That's either reassuring or unsettling depending on who made them.

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - What happens if the Echoes are fed too much? What's the upper limit?
  - Who maintains the notice board, and what else do they maintain?
    (The same small, tidy hand has written a note scratched beside the
    iron-banded door at the end of the gallery.)
  - What rustled behind the knee-height gap?
  - What happened to the Fletcher twins in the left passage?
    What is the "obligation"?
  - What is Marta's actual relationship to the dungeon?
  - What does "faster" mean in Marta's hint?
  - What is the green smell in the second chamber?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Gallery beyond the hexagonal antechamber, approaching
    an iron-banded oak door with a scratched note beside it. Alcove with
    unknown shelf object midway down the gallery (left wall).
  Inventory: belt knife, rope (coiled), dried meat, full waterskin,
    second-best boots
  Time position: Mid-morning, day one
  Active branch: main
  Key events so far:
    Ch 1: Arrived at dungeon entrance. Met Marta. Declined her wares.
      Faced the three-way split. Chose the main path.
    Ch 2: Passed through baker's root cellar (both chambers). Read the
      ledger (the green smell, noted). Entered proper dungeon. Reached
      hexagonal antechamber. Read the notice board. Feeling confident.
    Ch 3: Took the forward passage. Failed to not hum (said "right then"
      instead). Fed the Echoes. Now accompanied by ~12 Echoes replaying
      footsteps and nose-breathing. Reached the gallery's far end:
      iron-banded door with tidy-hand note; alcove with shelf object.

READER PROMISES
  - Each branch of the dungeon will be genuinely different in tone and content
  - Marta's cryptic "left's faster" will pay off if that path is taken
  - The Fletcher twins' "obligation" will be explained on that path
  - The Echoes have been encountered. The no-humming rule mattered. Their
    upper limit / what "too much feeding" does is still promised.
  - The notice-board correspondent(s) will be met or explained
  - The knee-height gap leads somewhere inhabited and worth visiting
  - The story will maintain its light register even when things go
    sideways — genre-consistent humor throughout
  - The ledger's "green smell" is planted; it will resurface
