STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Period mystery (Regency, industrial)
  Tone: Gritty industrial Regency. Cold, observational prose. Class tension foregrounded. Bleak when the material demands; restrained when it doesn't. Not Austen-parlour; closer to early Gaskell crossed with a procedural.
  Core premise: A young woman is found dead in the tail-race of a Lancashire cotton mill at the height of the 1812 Luddite unrest. A half-pay officer, pressed into duty as deputy to a sick magistrate, must determine whether this is murder, frame-breaker reprisal, or something the mill master himself has reason to bury.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Captain Edmund Reigh — Half-pay officer of an unnamed line regiment, lately returned from the Peninsula (Badajoz aftermath implied). Local-born; his late father held a small weaver-clothier farmstead above Halworth. Carries a stiffened right knee and a quiet, observational manner. Currently deputising for the bedridden magistrate Mr. Pickford. Voice anchor: spare, declarative, asks one question at a time and lets silence do the rest. Status: active.

  Josiah Pemberton — Master of Pemberton's Mill, Halworth. Roughly fifty, broad, high-coloured, eats well and sleeps badly. New money; the three chimneys above the river are his. Frightened — three weeks ago William Horsfall (referenced obliquely as "a man who looked like me" shot in Middleton) was murdered by Luddites; he believes himself a likely next target. Stopped his own wheel to preserve Esther Holt's body. Voice anchor: formal, controlled, lets exactly enough through to seem honest. Status: active.

  Mr. Pickford — Elderly local magistrate, abed with a quinsy. Offstage so far. Status: active (offstage).

  Sergeant Daws — NCO of the Cumberland militia detachment billeted in Halworth out of Manchester. Older than his rank suggests; recognises Reigh from somewhere. Voice anchor: short sentences, pushes back once and then obeys. Status: active.

  Esther Holt — Winder, second floor of Pemberton's. Found drowned in the tail-race at first light; visible ligature mark and thumb-bruise indicate murder. Lodged with her sister Susannah in Brigg Row. Status: deceased (Ch. 1).

  Susannah Holt — Esther's elder sister. Not yet onstage; sent for via Rev. Brearley. Status: active (offstage).

  Rev. Thomas Brearley — Methodist preacher in Halworth; the Holts' minister. Sent for to bring Susannah to identify her sister. Status: active (offstage).

  Pemberton's gateman — Unnamed so far. Asleep at his post; thrashed by Pemberton; swears the gate was locked at midnight and at five. Status: active (offstage).

WORLD RULES
  - Realist historical fiction. No supernatural, no anachronism.
  - Setting: Halworth, fictional Lancashire mill town on a tributary of (probably) the Calder. March 1812.
  - Luddite frame-breaking is at its peak in the north of England. The Westhoughton mill (Wroe) was burned the month before. William Horsfall was assassinated near Middleton three weeks before Ch. 1 (referenced obliquely; canon to this world).
  - Cumberland militia detachment is billeted in town under Sgt. Daws; martial law not declared but the air carries it.
  - Pemberton's Mill is recently expanded — three new chimneys in the past year. Cardroom and winding-frame floors are operating with a workforce including children (the cough Reigh hears).

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Returning soldier, returning country: Reigh sees a town transformed in his absence. The industrial revolution as a thing that happened *while he was away killing Frenchmen*.
  - Class and the law: a mill-girl murdered, a mill-master frightened. Whose death gets investigated and whose gets buried.
  - Fear as evidence: Pemberton's terror is itself a fact in the case, but it cuts both ways — does it make him innocent or convenient?

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - Who killed Esther Holt and why?
  - How did the killer get her into the mill yard if the gate was locked? (Or was it?)
  - Why was Esther — a winder, not a cardroom hand — at the mill before dawn?
  - Pemberton's stopping of the wheel: scrupulous, or staged?
  - The gateman's account: corroboration or alibi-construction?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Pemberton's Mill yard, Halworth, Lancashire
  Time position: Dawn, an early March morning, 1812. Frost still on the cobbles.
  Inventory/resources: Reigh has the magistrate's authority by deputisation, the militia detachment under Daws as muscle, and his own observational habits. No surgeon mentioned yet.
  Key events so far:
    Ch. 1 — Reigh summoned to Pemberton's Mill at dawn; finds Esther Holt drowned in the tail-race with ligature and thumb-bruise to the throat. Confirmed murder. Yard sealed; Brearley and Susannah Holt sent for.

READER PROMISES
  - This is a murder. It will be solved or deliberately not solved — not forgotten.
  - The Luddite/industrial-unrest backdrop is not scenery; it will be load-bearing on the case.
  - Reigh's local provenance (his father's old farmstead above the town) will matter.
  - The gateman's account will be tested.
  - Susannah Holt will become a real character, not a witness fixture.
  - Pemberton's fear is set up as ambiguous: payoff requires we eventually know whether he was right to be afraid, and of what.
