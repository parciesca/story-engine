CHAPTER PROPOSAL
  Chapter: 1
  Branch: main
  Follows from: Story opening
  This chapter accomplishes: Establishes Vera and her world (methodical, post-divorce, work as anesthetic), places her inside the condemned building, and ends on the discovery that someone else is here too — still warm tea, a footstep above.
  Key beats:
    1. Vera arrives at 4221 Breslow St, notes the access door lock has been cut (not pried — precise). Files it. Moves on.
    2. Interior: lobby, cut power, October grey. She begins her structural assessment — tapping concrete, photographing rebar exposure, reading the horizontal crack pattern in the east wall.
    3. The stairwell. She notices the dust on the stairs has been disturbed in places — not footprints exactly, but disruptions. She notes it professionally, keeps working.
    4. Third floor. A corner apartment, door open. Sleeping bag rolled neatly. Camp stove. A plastic bin of provisions. Weeks of habitation, organized.
    5. On the windowsill: an enamel mug. She picks it up. The tea inside is faintly warm.
    6. From the floor above — the unmistakable creak of a footstep.
  Pacing note: Slow build. The first half is almost purely procedural — let the technical texture do the atmospheric work. The intrusion of habitation should feel like something gradually coming into focus rather than a sudden reveal.
  Story bible implications: Introduce Vera, the building, and the unnamed occupant (status: unknown). Establish the central tension — what does Vera do with this?
  Turning point assessment: Routine (opening chapter, foundation-laying)
