STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Contemporary literary fiction
  Tone: Grounded, procedural, understated — pressure that builds from
    human-scale friction. The texture of professional life used as a lens
    for inner life. No genre mechanics; the tension is interpersonal
    and ethical.
  Core premise: Structural engineer Vera Nakashima is doing a routine
    pre-demolition assessment of a condemned apartment building when
    she discovers someone is still living inside. What she does next
    is the story.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Vera Nakashima — Structural engineer, 44. City contract work, pre-demolition
    assessments. Methodical to the point of self-protection. Went through a
    divorce about a year ago and has been using work as an anesthetic since.
    Voice anchor: She notices things precisely before she decides how to feel
    about them. Technical vocabulary is her comfort zone; emotion arrives late
    and sideways.
    Status: active

  The Occupant — Unknown. Living in apartment 3C at 4221 Breslow St.
    Organized, deliberate, careful — the sleeping bag is cinched at both ends,
    the supplies are sealed. Has been there for weeks. Has books. A Bible
    without a cover. Was on the fourth floor when Vera found the warm mug.
    Status: unknown (present in building, not yet encountered)

WORLD RULES
  - The story takes place in a mid-sized Midwestern American city in October.
  - 4221 Breslow Street: six-story reinforced concrete block apartment building,
    built 1963. Condemned August of this year following a fourth-floor fire
    that revealed systemic moisture damage and carbonation in the load-bearing
    columns. Demolition pending.
  - City contract process: Vera is an independent structural engineer under
    contract with the city's demolition program. She does the assessment,
    signs the form, files it. There is a process for reporting unauthorized
    occupants — she knows what it is. She knows what happens when she does.
  - Technical texture: carbonation in concrete = alkaline rot that compromises
    the rebar's passive protection layer; once the carbonation front reaches
    the rebar, corrosion begins and the concrete cracks along aggregate
    boundaries. A slow, invisible process. Assessable only by inspection.
    Tapping with an inspection hammer to find hollow pops.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Condemnation and what it means: the building has been declared beyond
    saving by official process. Vera is the instrument of that declaration.
    What does it mean to inhabit something that's been condemned?
  - The methodical and the human: Vera's procedural instinct as a coping
    mechanism; the moment it's not sufficient.
  - Slow damage: carbonation, a marriage, whatever has driven someone to
    live in a condemned building — damage that works quietly and reveals
    itself only when you look closely.

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - Who is the occupant and why are they here?
  - What does Vera do now? She has three obvious moves and none of them
    are neutral.
  - Her divorce: referenced but not explored. Something happened. Her
    work-as-anesthetic pattern suggests ongoing pain.

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Vera is on the third floor of 4221 Breslow Street.
    The occupant is on the fourth floor directly above her.
  Time position: Mid-morning, October. The mug is still warm. The occupant
    knows someone is in the building.
  Key events so far:
    Ch.1 — Vera arrives at condemned building, begins assessment, finds
      evidence of habitation in apartment 3C, picks up a still-warm mug,
      hears a footstep on the floor above.

READER PROMISES
  - The meeting between Vera and the occupant will happen.
  - Vera's assessment — professional and otherwise — will be complicated by
    this encounter.
  - The divorce will surface. Not as backstory dump — it will become relevant.
  - The building's structural fate is still in Vera's hands: she hasn't
    signed anything yet.
