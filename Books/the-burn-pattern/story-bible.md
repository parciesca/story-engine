STORY BIBLE
============

PREMISE & TONE
  Genre: Quiet procedural / literary fiction
  Tone: Grounded, observational, human-scale. The weight of ordinary expertise. What
    fire reveals — about a place, about people, about the person doing the looking.
    No chosen ones, no world-level stakes. A woman doing a difficult job carefully,
    and what gets complicated.
  Core premise: Fire cause-and-origin investigator Nora Vass is sent to Aldersham,
    Oregon to examine a large commercial insurance claim after a historic fish cannery
    burns overnight. What she finds in the char pattern won't leave her alone.

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  Nora Vass — Fire cause-and-origin investigator, special investigations unit for an
    insurance company, based Portland OR. Mid-40s. Methodical, low-affect in professional
    contexts, privately precise about her own reasoning. Has been wrong once, badly, seven
    years ago — cleared, but it settled in her. Same kit in the same pockets for eleven
    years. Divorced. Voice anchor: states what she observes, not what she concludes.
    Status: active.

  Denton Ayres — Local fire marshal, Aldersham. Late 50s, close to retirement. Has
    already decided the cause (electrical, distribution panel, east wall). Not hostile
    to Nora but not interested in a second opinion either. Used to rooms accommodating
    him. Voice anchor: brevity, light condescension, shrugs that mean things.
    Status: active.

  Soledad Reyes — Owner, Aldersham Fish and Processing Company. ~50. Third generation;
    the company was her grandfather's, then her mother's, then hers. Said 72 words in
    her fire department statement. Present at scene with attorney, composed, careful.
    Has a fresh burn on her right forearm, above the wrist. Voice anchor: measured,
    formal, says less than she could.
    Status: active.

  [Attorney, unnamed — present at scene with Reyes, shoes and posture signaling his role.
    Spoke very little. Name/function TBD.]

WORLD RULES
  - Story is set in Aldersham, a small coastal town in Oregon. Everything smells like
    the ocean eventually, which complicates accelerant detection.
  - Fire investigation methodology: scene walk before anything else; read floor burn, V-
    patterns, low burn indicators; photograph before touching; sample collection and
    vial labeling; separation of the three post-fire layers (pre-fire state, fire spread,
    suppression disturbance).
  - Insurance company sends investigators for claims above a threshold (this one: $2.3M).
  - Nora does solo passes first, doesn't share working theories with fire marshals until
    she has evidence. Professional discipline, not evasion.
  - Being wrong costs things. Nora was cleared in a prior case but it left a mark.

THEMATIC THREADS
  - Expertise as a form of loneliness — the precision that makes you good at the job
    creates a kind of internal distance
  - What fire destroys vs. what it reveals (the literal pattern of char as metaphor)
  - Caution vs. instinct: when do you trust what you smell?

UNRESOLVED TENSIONS
  - The drain char pattern doesn't track with Ayres's ignition point. Two ignition points?
    Or does one explain the other?
  - The synthetic smell under the brine — collected sample, lab results pending
  - Soledad Reyes's burn on her right forearm (fresh, above wrist, consistent with splatter)
  - Reyes's statement was 72 words. Notably short.
  - What was Reyes doing at 2am before coming to the scene at 2:30?

TIMELINE & STATE
  Current location: Aldersham, Oregon — exterior of the Aldersham Fish and Processing
    Company cannery, Nora in her rental car in the parking lot
  Time position: Morning after the fire (cannery burned overnight, fire called in ~2am)
  Key events so far:
    Ch.1 — Nora arrives at the Aldersham cannery. Meets fire marshal Ayres (verdict:
      electrical). Walks the scene solo. Finds anomalous char pattern at a floor drain
      that doesn't originate from Ayres's ignition point; collects sample. Meets owner
      Soledad Reyes (with attorney), gets nothing. Reviews photos in her car; notices
      a possible burn on Reyes's forearm in one image.

READER PROMISES
  - Something is off about this fire. The story has committed to that. Payoff required.
  - Nora's prior bad case (seven years ago) has been named — weight it carries will need
    to surface
  - Soledad Reyes is withholding something. The story has pointed at her. She must
    eventually be more than a suspect or she'll feel like a plant.
  - The drain sample: reader will expect to know what it is
